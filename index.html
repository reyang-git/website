About

I'm a Partner at Giant Leap, where I invest in founders building ambitious companies to solve our most pressing global challenges across climate, health, and people. I work closely with founders who are driven not only by commercial success, but by the opportunity to create meaningful, systems-level change at scale. To date, Giant Leap has invested in over 30 companies tackling some of the world's most complex problems.

My motivation for this work comes from a deep belief that capital, when deployed thoughtfully, can be a powerful force for good. I'm particularly drawn to founders who combine intellectual rigour with purpose, those willing to take on hard problems and build enduring businesses that improve outcomes for people and the planet.

Alongside my investing role, I served on the Advisory Board for the Victorian Government's Equity Investment Attraction Fund, supporting the growth of Victoria's innovation and investment ecosystem.

Previously, I was Chair of the Startup Network, a grassroots, non-profit organisation dedicated to supporting founders through every stage of their startup journey. Earlier in my career, I worked in management consulting and corporate finance, experiences that continue to shape my approach to strategy, governance, and long-term value creation.

I also co-founded a non-profit organisation focused on raising funds and awareness for Motor Neurone Disease, work that reinforced my commitment to impact-driven initiatives and purpose-led leadership.

The BOLD Contract

In 2019, I led the design of a pioneering impact-linked debt instrument - the BOLD (Beneficial Outcomes Linked Debt) contract - a novel financing tool that aligns investor returns with measurable positive social and environmental outcomes. Unlike traditional debt, the BOLD contract reduces the borrower's outstanding balance as their impact increases, effectively incentivising mission-driven businesses to achieve greater outcomes while providing impact-aligned investors with a differentiated risk-return profile. The first application of this instrument was a catalytic loan aimed at supporting a startup focused on empowering neurodiverse talent, demonstrating how innovative debt can simultaneously drive impact and commercial growth.
